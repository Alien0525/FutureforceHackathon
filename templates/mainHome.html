    Challenges faced by the DHH community



    Non-inclusive society

    Society is tailored according to the hearing community and thus not accessible to DHH community. Becoming aware and acknowledging issues faced the DHH community is the first step for creating a more inclusive environment.

    Communication Gap

    There is huge communication gap & hestitation between the two communities that can be overcome through a standardized sign language.

    Non-accessible learning resources

    Due to diverse languages and regions in India, there are many variations of sign languages. Hence, only in 2017, Indian Sign Language was official recognized to be a source of teaching in schools.

    Lack of practice

    There is a only a unidirectional mode of learning ISL, that is through videos. Getting fluent in any language requires practicing, a feature deliberately added in the forum to enhance sign skills.

    Frequently Asked Questioins

    • How can I become a volunteer?

      You can become a volunteer by simply signing up on our site. We will be sending out frequent mails to join up as a volunteer. The direct link on the site is yet to be added, though you can contribute directly on the site.

    • Is learning feature inclusive of both DHH and hearing community?

      Yes, the feature is inclusive and designed to serve customers of both the communities. Moreover, we have added both speech and text as input to learn ISL languages offering great user experience.

    • What is meant by Gamification of the App?

      Gamification of the App ensures that the user is rewarded with a sense of achievement that motivates them to continue learning ISL and be an active member of the community.
